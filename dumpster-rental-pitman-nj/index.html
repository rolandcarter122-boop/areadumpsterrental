Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best way to load the dumpster to maximize space?

Load it like you’re packing a moving truck. Place large, flat items against the walls first. Break down all boxes and furniture. Then, fill in the gaps with smaller debris. Don't just toss things in randomly. A strategically loaded dumpster can hold significantly more.

Can I put the dumpster on the street in front of my house?

In most municipalities, placing a roll off container on a public street requires a permit from the city or county. The responsibility for securing this permit lies with the customer. It's almost always easier and cheaper to place the dumpster on your private property if you have the space.

What if I have a mix of regular junk and heavy stuff like a little bit of concrete?

A small amount of concrete or brick can usually be mixed in with a regular load in our larger dumpsters. However, if you have more than a cubic yard of heavy debris, you will need a dedicated 10 yard roll off dumpster to stay within legal weight limits. Always tell us about heavy materials when you book.

How is the final weight of my dumpster actually determined?

Our driver takes the full rolloff box to a state-certified weigh station. The truck is weighed full, and then weighed again after it has been emptied. The difference between the two weights is the exact, certified tonnage of your debris, and you can request a copy of the scale ticket.

Are your rental periods firm, or can I get it picked up early?

Our rental periods are flexible. If you finish your project early, just give us a call! We are happy to schedule an early pickup to get the bin out of your way. You are not required to keep it for the full duration.

Pitman, NJ Insights: Population,
Zip Codes, Influence, and Service Areas

Pitman is a borough in Gloucester County, in the U.S. state of New Jersey. As of the 2020 United States census, the borough's population was 8,780, a decrease of 231 (−2.6%) from the 2010 census count of 9,011, which in turn reflected a decline of 320 (−3.4%) from the 9,331 in the 2000 census. The borough was named for Rev. Charles Pitman, a Methodist minister.
